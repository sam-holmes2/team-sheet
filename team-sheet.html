✦  WELCOME TO TEAM-SHEET

Your mind is made up of parts: the one that pushes forward, the one that shuts down, the one that numbs when things get hard. team-sheet maps your inner system as it grows through reflection. No prior IFS experience needed.

How do you want to use it? You can change this at any time.
A  Fill it in yourself. No AI required, works completely offline.
1
Go to the Parts tab and click "+ Add part". Start with whatever you notice most in yourself: a voice, a pattern, a recurring tension.
2
Give it a name and a role (Manager, Firefighter, or Exile). Fill in what you know: what it does, what it fears, when it shows up. No need to fill everything at once.
3
Open the Map tab to see your parts as a visual network. Add connections between parts in each part's Relationships section.
4
Export regularly using the Data button (bottom bar). Your data only exists in this browser, so Export is your backup.
B  Copy-paste with your AI. Journal in any AI, then import the result.
1
Download instructions.md, the file that tells your AI how team-sheet works.
2
Create a project in your AI of choice and upload instructions.md to its knowledge base.
3
Start a session. Try: "Here's what's been present for me lately..." or use a Session Starter from the Journal tab.
4
When done, ask your AI: "Update my data" and copy the output.
5
your data, paste the output, and hit Apply. Your map updates instantly.
C  Chat inside the app. API key or local Ollama.
1
Open Chat using the star (✦) icon in the bottom bar.
2
Open Chat Settings (gear icon inside the chat panel) and enter your API key, or connect a local Ollama model. The settings screen shows the exact Ollama start command for your OS.
3
Start a session. Journal for 10-20 minutes and your parts map fills in from your own words. No copy-pasting needed.
i

Journal with any AI
Use any AI app you already have. Copy and paste to update your map.
1
Download instructions.md. This tells your AI how team-sheet works and what data to produce.
2

Paste into Import to apply the update.

Your AI infers which parts were present, how relationships shifted, and whether any Self-led moments occurred. Lock any field to protect your own writing from being overwritten.

Before each new session, Export your latest data.json and paste it into your AI project so it has your current state.

⚙  SECURITY
⚠ Your data is stored only in this browser. Nothing leaves your machine unless you send it. If you journal with an AI, that provider may store what you share with them. A password encrypts your data in the browser. Use Firefox for the most isolated storage when running as a local file.
Password Protection

When set, all your data is strongly encrypted in the browser. Use a long random passphrase, or generate one with a password manager.

⚠ If you forget your password, your data cannot be recovered. Keep an export of your data.json as a backup.

Once a password is set, use in the toolbar to back up your encrypted data to a private GitHub repo.

↕  SYNC TO GITHUB

Back up your encrypted data to a private GitHub repo. Pull it on another device to restore.

A security password is required before you can use GitHub sync. This keeps your GitHub token encrypted alongside your data.

Create a fine-grained PAT with Contents read/write on your backup repo only.

Pull will merge the GitHub backup into your current data: new entries from GitHub are added, and any fields both versions changed are updated from the backup. You can undo the merge immediately after if needed.
On a new device: fill in your token and repository above, then pull. Your data will be merged in. Make sure your password in Security matches the one you used on your original device.

Push after each session to keep a backup accessible from another device.

Before you start

Your messages will be sent to your AI provider and are subject to their privacy policy. team-sheet never stores or sees your data.

IFS sessions can involve sensitive personal material. Use a provider and model you trust. For full privacy, use a local model via Ollama (no data leaves your device).
